Who I Am

I am a Software Engineering undergraduate specialising in Data Science at Daffodil International University, carrying a near-perfect CGPA of 3.995 / 4.00. Growing up in Dhaka, I was drawn to computer science not for its outputs — but for the questions it forces you to ask: whose problems does a model solve? Who does it fail? Can a clinician actually trust what it shows?

Those questions now shape my entire research direction. My undergraduate thesis investigates Trustworthy Multimodal Explainable AI for brain tumour classification — fusing multi-sequence MRI data with Grad-CAM saliency maps and SHAP value analysis to produce diagnostic outputs that are both accurate and genuinely legible to medical professionals. Accuracy alone is not enough when the stakes are a patient's life.

Beyond the lab, I have led cross-functional teams to two national competition selections — including the Final Round of AI BuildFest 2026, Bangladesh's largest AI competition with 610+ participating teams. I serve as Executive Member of the university's Data Science Club and have organised national events including the DIU Data Summit 2025 and National Robo Spark 2024.

I am actively seeking a fully funded international postgraduate scholarship — Erasmus Mundus, or equivalent MSc/PhD programmes in AI and Machine Learning — where I can deepen my research in trustworthy AI and contribute to a world-class academic environment. My long-term goal is to return to Bangladesh and strengthen local AI research capacity.
