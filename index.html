Undergraduate Thesis

Ongoing · 2026 – Present · Undergraduate Thesis

A Trustworthy Multimodal Deep Learning Framework Integrating Multi-Sequence MRI and Clinical Data with Explainable AI for Brain Tumor Classification

A comprehensive XAI-driven research initiative combining multi-sequence MRI modalities (T1, T1c, T2, FLAIR) with structured clinical metadata, harnessing the interpretive power of Grad-CAM, SHAP, and counterfactual reasoning to create diagnostic AI that clinicians can understand, audit, and trust.

Multimodal MRI Fusion

Integrates four MRI sequences — T1, T1-contrast, T2, and FLAIR — alongside structured clinical metadata. Feature-level and decision-level fusion strategies are evaluated to determine which combination yields the richest diagnostic signal, going beyond single-modality limitations prevalent in existing literature.

T1 · T1c · T2 · FLAIR
🔍
Explainable AI Integration

Deploys Grad-CAM for spatial attention heatmaps, SHAP for feature-level attribution, and counterfactual reasoning to answer "what-if" clinical questions. Every prediction is accompanied by a visual and quantitative explanation, enabling radiologists to validate and audit the model's decision pathway.

Grad-CAM · SHAP · Counterfactuals
📊
Uncertainty Quantification

Incorporates Bayesian deep learning and Monte Carlo Dropout to estimate model confidence on a per-prediction basis. Low-confidence predictions trigger a human review flag rather than a final classification — a critical safety mechanism for clinical deployment where incorrect high-confidence errors carry severe consequences.

Bayesian DL · MC Dropout
🏥
Clinical Trustworthiness

Beyond performance metrics, this research rigorously evaluates clinical alignment: do the explanations highlight diagnostically meaningful regions? Are predictions calibrated and robust under data shift? The goal is a framework that meets the transparency and reliability bar required for real-world healthcare deployment.

Calibration · Robustness · Safety
