About Me

Building Trustworthy AI For A Better World

I'm a Software Engineering undergraduate majoring in Data Science at Daffodil International University, maintaining a near-perfect CGPA of 3.995 / 4.00. I'm deeply passionate about making AI that is not just accurate — but interpretable, fair, and clinically deployable.

My current undergraduate thesis, focused on Trustworthy Multimodal XAI for brain tumor classification, merges multi-sequence MRI analysis with explainability frameworks like Grad-CAM and SHAP to build diagnostic AI that clinicians can actually trust.

I believe the most impactful AI breakthroughs aren't just technically brilliant — they're built with human dignity and transparency at their core.

Research

Undergraduate Thesis

Ongoing · 2026 – Present
Undergraduate Thesis

A Trustworthy Multimodal Deep Learning Framework Integrating Multi-Sequence MRI And Clinical Data With Explainable AI For Brain Tumor Classification

Research Objectives

  • Proposing a trustworthy multimodal deep learning architecture combining MRI sequences (T1, T1c, T2, FLAIR) and clinical metadata.
  • Applying feature fusion strategies for improved diagnostic modeling performance.
  • Integrating Grad-CAM, SHAP & counterfactual reasoning to enhance interpretability and clinical reliability.
  • Developing AI systems aligned with real-world clinical deployment constraints.

Key Focus Areas

  • Trustworthy AI in healthcare — bridging model performance and clinical interpretability
  • Multimodal data fusion integrating multi-sequence MRI and clinical features
  • Explainable AI using Grad-CAM, SHAP, and counterfactual explanations
  • Uncertainty quantification for confidence-aware clinical decision support
