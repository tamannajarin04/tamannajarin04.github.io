About Me

Crafting Trustworthy AI for Human Benefit

I'm a Software Engineering undergraduate majoring in Data Science at Daffodil International University, maintaining a near-perfect CGPA of 3.995/4.00. My passion lies in making AI that is not just accurate — but interpretable, reliable, and socially responsible.

My current thesis, TruBrain-XAI, focuses on multimodal deep learning for brain tumor classification, integrating uncertainty quantification and explainable AI to support transparent clinical decision-making.

Undergraduate Thesis Work

Ongoing · 2026–Present
Undergraduate Thesis

A Trustworthy Multimodal Deep Learning Framework Integrating Multi-Sequence MRI and Clinical Data with Explainable AI for Brain Tumor Classification

Research Objectives

  • Proposing a trustworthy multimodal deep learning architecture combining MRI sequences (T1, T1c, T2, FLAIR) and clinical metadata.
  • Applying feature fusion strategies for improved diagnostic modeling.
  • Integrating explainability techniques (Grad-CAM, SHAP, counterfactual reasoning) to enhance interpretability and clinical reliability.
  • Aimed at developing transparent and clinically aligned AI systems for real-world deployment.

Key Focus Areas

  • Trustworthy AI in healthcare — bridging model performance and clinical interpretability
  • Multimodal data fusion integrating multi-sequence MRI and clinical features
  • Explainable AI using Grad-CAM, SHAP, and counterfactual explanations for transparent decision-making
