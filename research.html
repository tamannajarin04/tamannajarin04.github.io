Thesis Overview

A Trustworthy Multimodal Deep Learning Framework Integrating Multi-Sequence MRI and Clinical Data with Explainable AI for Brain Tumor Classification

Ongoing · 2026 – Present
Abstract

Combines T1, T1-contrast, T2, and FLAIR MRI modalities with structured clinical metadata. Feature-level and decision-level fusion strategies are evaluated alongside Grad-CAM spatial heatmaps, SHAP attributions, and counterfactual reasoning — producing predictions that are not only accurate but transparent and clinically auditable. Bayesian deep learning and Monte Carlo Dropout further provide calibrated uncertainty estimates, ensuring low-confidence cases are flagged for human review.

Research Components
Multimodal MRI Fusion

Integrates four MRI sequences — T1, T1-contrast, T2, and FLAIR — alongside structured clinical metadata. Feature-level and decision-level fusion strategies are evaluated to determine which combination yields the richest diagnostic signal for tumor classification.

T1 T1c T2 FLAIR Clinical Metadata
Explainable AI Integration

Deploys Grad-CAM for spatial attention heatmaps, SHAP for feature-level attribution, and counterfactual reasoning to answer "what-if" clinical questions. Every prediction is paired with visual and quantitative explanations reviewable by clinicians.

Grad-CAM SHAP Counterfactuals
Uncertainty Quantification

Incorporates Bayesian deep learning and Monte Carlo Dropout to estimate model confidence per prediction. Low-confidence cases trigger mandatory human review — a critical safety mechanism for responsible clinical deployment.

Bayesian DL MC Dropout Calibration
Clinical Trustworthiness

Beyond accuracy metrics, this work rigorously evaluates whether explanations highlight diagnostically meaningful regions and whether predictions remain calibrated under data shift — the transparency and robustness bar required for real-world healthcare deployment.
